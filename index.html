Robust Symmetry Detection via Riemannian Langevin Dynamics

Jihyeon Je1, Jack Liu1, Guandao Yang1, Boyang Deng1, Or Litany2, Leo Guibas1
1Stanford University 2Technion
Paper arXiv Code

In this work, we present a new perspective of understanding symmetries, which leads to the adoption of Riemannian Langevin dynamics to symmetry detection.

Abstract

From natural to artificial objects, geometric structures possessing partial or total symmetries are ubiquitous. Detecting these symmetries is a fundamental and long-standing challenge in geometry processing, as symmetries inform not only the structure but also the semantics, aesthetics, and function of objects. In this work, we bring a novel perspective to this challenge, connecting this classic problem to recent progresses in generative models.

In particular, we interpret the problem of detecting symmetry in a transformation space as a mode seeking problem in a score field. This probabilistic formulation allows us to introduce elegant approaches from generative models, such as Langevin dynamics, to symmetry detection. As a score field, the transformation space of a shape is less sensitive to noise than the Euclidean space. Meanwhile, unlike mean-shift clustering used in prior detection methods, Langevin dynamics can robustly discover the modes even when the score field is noisy. Consequently, our solution accurately detects symmetries of different strengths even on noisy shapes, which greatly enriches the applications of symmetry detection. We show that our solution could be applied to a wider range of shapes, evidencing the value of a more robust symmetry detection algorithm in geometry processing.
